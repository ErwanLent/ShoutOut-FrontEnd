ShoutOut Launches For iPhone

ShoutOut, a location based chatroom app just launched for iPhone. ShoutOut is an innovative communication application that allows users to network and connect with people who are in their immediate vicinity. They aim to disrupt hyperlocal communication and reshape the way we think about relationships with strangers and friends around us. The first target on their list is college and high school campuses, places where apps such as Facebook and SnapChat have spread virally in the past.

After joining the Brandery Accelerator in June of 2014 and securing their seed stage funding, ShoutOut moved from Isla Vista to Cincinnati, where the Brandery is located. The Brandery is a prominent seed fund, widely known as one of the top 10 startup accelerators in the world.

"I'm honored to be working alongside the Brandery and its long list of mentors. The real-life experience that the Brandery brings to the table will be critical in the expansion of ShoutOut.” says Erwan Lent, CTO of ShoutOut. “Our goal is to continue to break down barriers that prevent people from interacting and networking with one another. We want to create a mobile experience that allows our users to interact with their environment in a whole new way,” adds Joel Green, CEO.

Co-Founders Joel Green and Erwan Lent began working on ShoutOut in early 2014. Green previously worked for the University of California, Santa Barbara as an undergraduate researcher developing automated lab tools, and performing big data analysis. Lent also worked at the University of California, Santa Barbara as a full stack developer, architecting highly available systems needed for distributing large scaled web applications.

Both founders dropped out of high school working as software engineers, and met as members of the SBCC ACM programming team, where they won first place, ranking SBCC internationally as the number one two-year college. They went on to win first and third place prizes at the two biggest hackathons on the west coast - LAHacks and HackTech. They proceeded to drop out of UCSB to continue building ShoutOut, which was a side project at the time.

With ShoutOut launched for iPhone and coming soon to Android, they continue gearing up for Brandery’s October 15th demo day where they plan to raise a Series A and make additional full time hires.
